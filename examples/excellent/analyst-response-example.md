# Excellent analyst response example

*An example of a strong analyst relations response framing. Claims are illustrative and must be verified before use.*

---

## Response framing: Addressing enterprise readiness questions

The concern: [Analyst] noted in their recent report that Arize is a notable player in AI observability but raised questions about enterprise readiness.

The opportunity: Provide specific, verifiable evidence of enterprise deployment, security posture, and integration depth.

---

## Recommended framing

Arize is in production at enterprise customers across [industries to be confirmed]. The platform handles [volume to be confirmed] at scale, with [data residency and security controls to be confirmed with legal].

The integration layer is built on OpenTelemetry. That matters for enterprise customers because it means they can bring existing instrumentation, route data through existing collectors, and avoid vendor lock-in at the data layer.

On evaluation: Arize supports both offline and online evaluation workflows. Enterprise teams use offline evals to test against golden datasets before deployment, and online evals to monitor quality in production. Both are accessible through the same interface and API.

We would welcome the opportunity to walk through a technical proof of concept with your team or connect you with a customer reference.

---

## What makes this strong

- Addresses the analyst's specific concern directly
- Uses precise technical language (OpenTelemetry, offline/online evals, golden datasets)
- Acknowledges what requires verification rather than asserting it
- Proposes a concrete next step (proof of concept, customer reference)
- Avoids overclaiming
