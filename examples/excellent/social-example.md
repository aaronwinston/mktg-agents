# Excellent social example

*Examples of strong LinkedIn and X posts on AI observability topics.*

---

## LinkedIn (technical founder POV)

Most teams instrument their agents. Few teams actually look at the traces.

The trace is where you find out what your agent actually did. Not what you think it did. Not what it did in your test environment. What it did with that specific input, in that specific session, when it returned an unexpected result.

Traces answer the questions that logs do not. Which tool was called and why. What the model received vs. what it produced. Where in the chain the problem started.

If you are debugging an agent and you are not starting with the trace, you are starting in the wrong place.

---

## X (concise technical take)

The hardest part of debugging agents is not finding the bug. It is knowing where to look.

Traces give you the full picture: every tool call, every model input, every step in the chain. Start there.

---

## LinkedIn (company account)

Agents fail in ways that logs do not capture.

A trace shows you what actually happened. The tool that was called but returned an error. The prompt that was constructed differently than expected. The step where the agent went off-script.

Teams that debug with traces fix problems faster than teams that guess.

## What makes these strong

- Lead with a specific observation, not a generic claim
- Use technical terms precisely (traces, tool calls, spans)
- The reader learns something in the first two lines
- No hype language
- Developer voice, not brand voice
