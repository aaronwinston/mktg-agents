# Style Guide

## Sentence style

Use short, direct sentences.

Prefer: "Teams need to know why an agent failed."

Avoid: "In today's fast-moving AI landscape, teams are increasingly looking for ways to better understand the underlying reasons why their autonomous systems may not be performing as intended."

## Paragraphs

Keep paragraphs short. One idea per paragraph. Use bullets when they improve clarity.

## Headings

Use headings that tell the reader what they will learn.

Prefer:
- "How evals catch regressions"
- "Why tracing matters for agents"
- "What to measure before production"

Avoid:
- "Introduction"
- "Unlocking value"
- "A better way forward"

## Technical examples

Use examples when possible. Examples can include:
- Agent steps and tool calls
- Evaluation criteria
- Failure modes
- Before and after outputs
- Trace inspection
- Regression analysis
- Prompt or system instruction changes

## No em dashes

Use commas, periods, semicolons, or parentheses instead of em dashes.

## No filler openings

Avoid:
- In today's world
- As AI continues to evolve
- Now more than ever
- It goes without saying
- There is no doubt that

## Preferred structure for technical blogs

1. Problem
2. Why it matters
3. Technical concept
4. Workflow or example
5. How to evaluate
6. Common failure modes
7. Practical takeaway

## Preferred structure for launch copy

1. What changed
2. Who it helps
3. Why it matters
4. How it works
5. How to get started
6. Proof or context
7. CTA

## Preferred structure for social posts

1. Hook
2. Specific observation
3. Why it matters
4. Practical detail
5. CTA or question
