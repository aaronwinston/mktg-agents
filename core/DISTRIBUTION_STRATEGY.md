# Distribution Strategy

Every major content asset should have a distribution plan before drafting is complete.

## Distribution channels

### Blog
Use for: search-driven education, product education, technical explainers, thought leadership, launch narratives.

### LinkedIn
Use for: founder POV, executive narratives, launch amplification, customer proof, analyst credibility, category framing.

### X
Use for: technical hooks, founder POV, developer community discussion, research snippets, fast reactions to market news.

### Newsletter
Use for: curated education, product updates, event follow-up, recurring series content.

### Sales enablement
Use for: buyer guides, customer proof, analyst excerpts, objection handling, use case narratives.

### Partner channels
Use for: joint launches, ecosystem credibility, co-marketing, technical integrations.

## Repurposing model

A strong source asset should produce:
- 1 blog post
- 1 executive LinkedIn post
- 1 company LinkedIn post
- 2 to 5 X posts
- 1 newsletter module
- 1 sales blurb
- 1 internal summary
- 3 short pull quotes
- 1 visual or diagram brief

## Distribution checklist

Before publishing, define:
- Primary audience
- Primary channel
- Secondary channels
- CTA
- Internal stakeholders who need to know
- Launch timing and embargo status
- Social variants and owner
- Sales or partner use case
- Measurement plan (what does success look like at 30 days?)
