## Overview

This runbook defines the process for creating in-depth strategic briefs that inform major business decisions, market entries, product pivots, or competitive responses. These are high-stakes documents requiring deep research and validation.

**When to use:** Quarterly strategic reviews, market entry decisions, competitive response planning, product strategy updates, organizational pivots

**Prerequisites:**
- Clear strategic question or planning scope defined
- Stakeholder alignment on objectives
- Research budget allocated
- Timeline agreed upon

## Workflow Steps

### Phase 1: Scoping & Research Planning (1-2 days)

#### Step 1.1: Strategic Objective Definition (Strategist)
**Duration:** 1 day

**Responsibilities:**
- Define the core strategic question or decision
- Identify stakeholders and approval requirements
- Establish research boundaries and scope
- Define success criteria and key questions to answer
- Create research roadmap

**Inputs:**
- Business trigger or question
- Strategic context
- Stakeholder list
- Time and budget constraints

**Outputs:**
- Strategic brief scope document:
  - Core question and decision frame
  - Stakeholder requirements
  - Research areas (primary, secondary, tertiary)
  - Research methods
  - Approval workflow
  - Success criteria
- Research roadmap
- Stakeholder communications plan

**QA Gate:** Scope reviewed and approved by key stakeholders

#### Step 1.2: Research Plan & Source Identification (Researcher)
**Duration:** 1 day (concurrent)

**Responsibilities:**
- Identify research sources (primary, secondary, tertiary)
- Create detailed research plan
- Identify expert interviews needed
- Plan data collection approach
- Set research milestones

**Inputs:**
- Strategic scope document
- Stakeholder requirements
- Internal data access permissions

**Outputs:**
- Detailed research plan:
  - Primary research (interviews, surveys, focus groups)
  - Secondary research (reports, articles, studies)
  - Internal data analysis (customer data, product metrics)
  - Expert sources and interview targets
  - Timeline and resource requirements

**QA Gate:** Research plan feasibility assessed

### Phase 2: Research & Analysis (4-6 days)

#### Step 2.1: Primary Research (Researcher)
**Duration:** 2-3 days

**Responsibilities:**
- Conduct expert interviews (5-10 key sources)
- Facilitate stakeholder workshops if needed
- Analyze survey responses (if applicable)
- Document findings and quotes
- Synthesize interview insights

**Inputs:**
- Interview targets and questions
- Survey instruments (if applicable)
- Interview protocols
- Recording/note-taking tools

**Outputs:**
- Interview notes and recordings
- Key quotes and insights
- Interview synthesis report
- Data patterns and themes identified
- Recommendations from experts

**QA Gate:** Interview coverage met, notes documented comprehensively

#### Step 2.2: Secondary Research & Analysis (Analyst)
**Duration:** 2-3 days (concurrent)

**Responsibilities:**
- Collect relevant reports, articles, research studies
- Analyze competitive landscape
- Track market trends and developments
- Synthesize industry best practices
- Create data summaries and visualizations

**Inputs:**
- Research plan
- Competitive intelligence requirements
- Market analysis frameworks
- Internal benchmarking data

**Outputs:**
- Competitive analysis document
- Market trend summary
- Industry benchmark report
- Secondary source summary (key findings)
- Data visualizations (charts, graphs)
- Curated source bibliography

**QA Gate:** Sourcing verification (reputable sources), data accuracy checked

#### Step 2.3: Internal Data Analysis (Analyst)
**Duration:** 1-2 days (concurrent)

**Responsibilities:**
- Analyze relevant internal data
- Create performance dashboards
- Identify trends and patterns
- Benchmark against historical performance
- Synthesize findings

**Inputs:**
- Internal data access
- Data queries or reports
- Performance metrics
- Customer/product data

**Outputs:**
- Internal data analysis report
- Performance dashboards
- Historical trend analysis
- Customer segment analysis
- Opportunity identification
- Risk assessment from internal data

**QA Gate:** Data accuracy verified, methodology documented

### Phase 3: Synthesis & Draft (2-3 days)

#### Step 3.1: Strategic Analysis & Synthesis (Strategist + Analyst)
**Duration:** 1-2 days

**Responsibilities:**
- Synthesize findings across all research streams
- Create strategic framework or model
- Identify key insights and implications
- Develop strategic options/recommendations
- Assess risks and opportunities

**Inputs:**
- Interview findings
- Secondary research analysis
- Internal data analysis
- Strategic frameworks (SWOT, Porter's 5 Forces, etc.)

**Outputs:**
- Strategic analysis document:
  - Executive summary (1-2 pages)
  - Key findings (3-5 major insights)
  - Strategic implications
  - Options analysis (2-4 strategic paths)
  - Recommendation with rationale
  - Risk assessment and mitigation
  - Success metrics for chosen path
  - Implementation considerations

**QA Gate:** Logic and reasoning verified, recommendations supported by evidence

#### Step 3.2: Strategic Brief Writing (Strategist)
**Duration:** 1 day

**Responsibilities:**
- Write comprehensive strategic brief
- Structure findings for executive audience
- Create executive summary
- Develop supporting narratives
- Prepare appendices and detailed analysis
- Create compelling visualizations

**Inputs:**
- Strategic analysis
- Research findings
- Data visualizations
- Strategic frameworks
- Style guides

**Outputs:**
- Complete strategic brief (15-30 pages typically):
  - Title and metadata
  - Executive summary
  - Key findings and implications
  - Strategic options analysis
  - Recommendation with rationale
  - Risk assessment
  - Success metrics and KPIs
  - Implementation roadmap
  - Appendices with supporting data
  - Source citations

**QA Gate:** Draft completeness checked, structure and flow reviewed

### Phase 4: Review & Validation (2-3 days)

#### Step 4.1: Editorial Review (Editor)
**Duration:** 1 day

**Responsibilities:**
- Review for clarity and accessibility
- Ensure logical flow and organization
- Check for consistency and redundancy
- Improve readability for executive audience
- Verify citations and sources

**Inputs:**
- Strategic brief draft
- Brand guidelines
- Audience profile

**Outputs:**
- Edited brief with comments
- Readability assessment
- Structural recommendations
- Citation verification report

**QA Gate:** Editorial standards met, all edits justified

#### Step 4.2: Strategic Validation (Validator)
**Duration:** 1-2 days

**Responsibilities:**
- Validate strategic soundness
- Check recommendation logic
- Assess evidence quality and sufficiency
- Review for biases or gaps
- Validate assumptions

**Inputs:**
- Edited strategic brief
- Research source documentation
- Strategic frameworks
- Business context

**Outputs:**
- Validation report:
  - Evidence quality assessment
  - Logical soundness rating
  - Assumption documentation
  - Potential biases identified
  - Coverage gaps (if any)
  - Recommendation for approval/revision
  - Sign-off if approved

**QA Gate:** Strategic soundness verified, validator approval obtained

#### Step 4.3: Author Finalization (Strategist)
**Duration:** 1 day

**Responsibilities:**
- Incorporate editorial changes
- Address validation concerns
- Finalize formatting and design
- Create presentation version if needed
- Prepare executive summary for standalone use

**Inputs:**
- Editorial comments
- Validation report
- Design specifications

**Outputs:**
- Final strategic brief (approved version)
- Executive summary (standalone 1-2 page version)
- Presentation deck (optional, 20-30 slides)
- Final QA checklist sign-off

**QA Gate:** All changes incorporated, final approval obtained

## Strategic Brief Structure Template

```
1. TITLE PAGE & METADATA
   - Title, date, author, approval signatures

2. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (1-2 pages)
   - Core question
   - Key findings (3-5 bullets)
   - Recommendation
   - Timeline and next steps

3. STRATEGIC CONTEXT (2-3 pages)
   - Background and trigger for analysis
   - Scope and objectives
   - Methodology overview
   - Stakeholders and approval path

4. MARKET & COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS (4-6 pages)
   - Market size, growth, trends
   - Competitive landscape
   - Key competitor analysis
   - Market dynamics and forces
   - Opportunities and threats

5. INTERNAL ASSESSMENT (3-4 pages)
   - Current capabilities and performance
   - Strategic strengths and weaknesses
   - Customer feedback and needs
   - Organizational readiness

6. STRATEGIC OPTIONS (4-6 pages)
   - Option 1: Description, pros, cons, resource requirements
   - Option 2: Description, pros, cons, resource requirements
   - Option 3: Description, pros, cons, resource requirements
   - Option comparison matrix

7. RECOMMENDATION (2-3 pages)
   - Recommended path
   - Rationale and evidence
   - Expected outcomes and success metrics
   - Risk assessment and mitigation
   - Resource requirements

8. IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP (2-3 pages)
   - Phase 1: Month 1-3
   - Phase 2: Month 4-6
   - Phase 3: Month 7-12
   - Milestones and checkpoints
   - Success metrics and KPIs

9. APPENDICES
   - Detailed competitive analysis
   - Interview summaries
   - Data and visualizations
   - Source bibliography
   - Methodology details
```

## Agent Sequence

```
Strategist (Scoping)
├─ Researcher (Research Planning)
    └─ Researcher (Primary Research)
        ├─ Analyst (Secondary Research)
        └─ Analyst (Internal Analysis)
            └─ Strategist + Analyst (Synthesis)
                └─ Strategist (Brief Writing)
                    └─ Editor (Editorial Review)
                        └─ Validator (Strategic Validation)
                            └─ Strategist (Finalization)
```

## Success Criteria

- Research completeness: >95% of planned research completed
- Evidence quality: All recommendations supported by 3+ sources
- Strategic soundness: Validator approval obtained
- Executive clarity: Brief understandable to C-suite audience
- Timeline compliance: Delivered on agreed date
- Stakeholder alignment: >80% stakeholder satisfaction

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Research delays | 20% time buffer built in, prioritization framework if needed |
| Interview unavailability | Secondary sources identified, alternative experts identified |
| Conflicting viewpoints | Document multiple perspectives, rely on evidence |
| Scope creep | Strict boundaries maintained, escalate to stakeholder if expansion needed |
| Stakeholder misalignment | Regular stakeholder updates, approval checkpoints |
