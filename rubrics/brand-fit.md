# Brand fit rubric

## Score definitions

### Score 5 (Excellent)
The content sounds exactly like the brand: technical, direct, useful, evidence-led. No hype language. No generic AI marketing. Claims are specific. The reader would associate this with a credible technical company.

### Score 3 (Acceptable)
The content is mostly on-brand but has one or two hype phrases or generic sentences. The core message is right but execution could be sharper.

### Score 1 (Poor)
The content sounds like generic AI marketing. It uses hype language, vague benefit claims, or sounds like a competitor could have written it.

## Reviewer questions

- Could this content have been written by a competitor?
- Does it sound like the brand voice in core/VOICE.md?
- Is there any hype language from the banned list?
- Does it make specific claims or vague ones?
- Would a technical founder be proud to put their name on this?

## Common failure modes

- Hype language from core/VOICE.md banned list
- Generic AI marketing language
- Executive framing instead of builder framing
- Vague benefit claims instead of specific product behavior
- Content that could apply to any AI company
