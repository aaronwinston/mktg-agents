# Analyst readiness rubric

## Score definitions

### Score 5 (Excellent)
Every claim is sourced or flagged. The category framing is clear. Proof points are concrete. The response or briefing addresses the analyst's specific concern. Executive review is confirmed.

### Score 3 (Acceptable)
Most claims are sourced. Category framing is present but could be stronger. Some proof points are vague.

### Score 1 (Poor)
Claims are unsourced. Category framing is missing or incorrect. No concrete proof points. Not ready for analyst communication.

## Reviewer questions

- Is every factual claim sourced or flagged for review?
- Is the category framing precise?
- Are proof points concrete and approved?
- Does the response address the analyst's actual concern?
- Has the executive or AR lead reviewed this?

## Common failure modes

- Unsourced competitive or benchmark claims
- Vague category language
- Missing proof points
- Not addressing the analyst's specific concern
- No executive review
