# SEO quality rubric

## Score definitions

### Score 5 (Excellent)
The title includes the primary keyword naturally. The meta description is under 155 characters and makes a clear promise. Headings align with search intent. Internal links are included. FAQ opportunities are addressed.

### Score 3 (Acceptable)
The title includes the keyword but is not optimized for click-through. Meta description is present but generic. Some headings align with search intent.

### Score 1 (Poor)
No keyword alignment. No meta description. Headings do not reflect search intent. No internal links.

## Reviewer questions

- Does the title include the primary keyword naturally?
- Is the meta description under 155 characters?
- Do headings match how developers search for this topic?
- Are there internal linking opportunities to existing content?
- Are FAQ-style questions addressed in the content?

## Common failure modes

- Keyword stuffing that sounds unnatural
- Meta descriptions that do not make a clear promise
- Headings that are creative but not searchable
- No internal links
- Ignoring search intent in favor of brand messaging
