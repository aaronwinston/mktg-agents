# Editorial quality rubric

## Score definitions

### Score 5 (Excellent)
The piece has a clear POV, a logical structure, a strong opening, and a memorable close. Every section earns its place. The reader comes away knowing something they can act on.

### Score 3 (Acceptable)
The piece has a clear argument but some sections are underdeveloped. The opening is functional but not compelling. The structure holds but could be tighter.

### Score 1 (Poor)
The piece lacks a clear POV. Sections do not connect. The opening is generic. The reader learns nothing specific.

## Reviewer questions

- Does the opening earn the reader's attention?
- Is the core argument clear by the end of the first section?
- Does every section advance the argument?
- Is the closing strong, or does it trail off?
- Would a smart reader share this with a colleague?

## Common failure modes

- No clear POV
- Generic opening that could apply to any topic
- Sections that do not connect
- Ending that trails off with a vague CTA
- Content that could have been written without product knowledge
