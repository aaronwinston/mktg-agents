## Package Overview

The Launch Campaign Package coordinates a major product or feature launch across all marketing channels. It delivers a unified message, coordinated timing, and maximum impact to drive awareness, consideration, and conversion.

**Business Objective:**
- Create market awareness for new offering
- Drive trial/adoption
- Generate qualified leads
- Establish competitive positioning

**Target Audience:** Key customer segments, prospects, analysts, influencers

**Distribution Channels:** 
- Email (owned: 2M+ subscribers)
- Social (4 platforms, 100K+ followers)
- Paid (LinkedIn, Google, Meta - $10-50K budget)
- PR (50-100 targets)
- Influencer (5-10 partnerships)
- Owned (blog, website, landing page)

**Expected Impact:** 
- 500K+ reach across channels
- 5%+ engagement rate on social
- 3%+ CTR on email
- 100+ MQL conversions
- $500K-5M revenue (depends on offer)

## Content Components

### 1. Core Launch Content

#### Landing Page
**Format:** Single-page HTML (CMS or builder)  
**Length:** 1500-2000 words + visual  
**Sections:**
- Hero: Eye-catching headline, subheading, CTA
- Problem statement: What challenge does this solve?
- Solution overview: How it works (high level)
- Key benefits: 3-5 main benefits with icons
- Feature highlights: 5-8 key features
- Use cases: 2-3 customer scenarios
- Comparison: vs. existing solution/competitors (optional)
- Testimonial/social proof: 1-2 customer quotes
- CTA section: Trial, demo, or purchase button
- FAQ: 5-8 common questions
- Footer: Legal, support links

**Specifications:**
- Mobile responsive, fast loading
- Conversion-optimized (minimal navigation)
- Video embed (product demo, 1-2 min)
- Form for email capture
- Multiple CTAs (learn more, try now, contact sales)
- SEO optimized (keywords, schema markup)

#### Launch Announcement Blog Post
**Format:** Blog post (1500-2000 words)  
**Content:**
- What was launched (product/feature, release date)
- Problem it solves
- How it works (medium detail)
- Why now (market context)
- Customer benefits
- Use cases and scenarios
- Pricing/availability
- Get started CTA
- FAQ or additional resources

**Specifications:**
- Published day of launch
- Cross-linked with landing page
- Author: Founder/product lead for credibility
- Embedded video
- Related resources section

#### Press Release
**Format:** Traditional PR format (500-750 words)  
**Content:**
- Lead paragraph: What, when, why
- Customer quote: Validating the launch
- Product details: Features, benefits, usage
- Availability: Pricing, timing, where to get
- Company boilerplate
- Contact information

**Specifications:**
- Distributed via PR Newswire or equivalent
- Format for traditional media
- Embargoed until launch date
- PDF and HTML versions

### 2. Email Campaign Content

#### Launch Announcement Email
**Format:** HTML email  
**Length:** 300-400 words  
**Content:**
- Subject: Announce the launch, create curiosity
- Greeting: Personal to segment if possible
- Hook: Why they should care
- What's new: Key benefits (not features)
- Visual: Product screenshot or demo
- CTA button: "Try Now" or "Learn More"
- PS: Limited-time offer or bonus (if applicable)

**Specs:**
- A/B test 3 subject lines
- Mobile responsive
- Single focused CTA
- Segmented by audience (customer vs. prospect)

#### Educational Email Series (3-5 emails)
**Format:** HTML email sequence (5 days post-launch)  
**Content:**
- Email 1: "Getting Started" guide
- Email 2: "Top 3 Use Cases"
- Email 3: "Pro Tips" or advanced feature walkthrough
- Email 4: Case study or customer success story
- Email 5: Limited-time offer or closing CTA

**Specs:**
- One email per day
- Progressive information reveal
- Varied CTAs (learn, try, buy, refer)
- Unsubscribe option (required)

### 3. Social Media Content

#### LinkedIn Campaign
**Content:** 15-20 posts over 4 weeks
- Launch announcement post (1500 chars)
- Employee spotlight posts (team behind feature)
- Use case posts (2-3 per week)
- Customer testimonial posts (1-2)
- Thought leadership (market context)
- Engagement hooks (polls, questions)

**Format:** Mix of:
- Text posts with image
- Document carousel (3-5 slides)
- Short video clips
- LinkedIn articles (repurposed blog)

**Timing:** M/W/F post schedule, Tuesday-Thursday peak times

#### Twitter Campaign
**Content:** 20-25 tweets over 4 weeks
- Launch thread (5-8 tweets)
- Quick tips (2x per week)
- Stats/facts about problem
- Quote graphics
- Engagement questions
- Reply to relevant conversations

**Format:**
- Text tweets (280 chars)
- Thread tweets (linked)
- Link to landing page
- Quote graphics or screenshots

**Timing:** Daily or near-daily tweets, 9am-1pm windows

#### Instagram Campaign
**Content:** Visual-first content
- Product hero image (high quality)
- Carousel: "5 ways this helps you"
- Reels: 15-30 second demo video
- Stories: 5-7 day story takeover
- Testimonial graphics
- Behind-the-scenes content

**Specs:**
- Professional photography
- Brand color palette
- Hashtag strategy (20-30 tags)
- Instagram Bio link to landing page

#### TikTok/Short Video
**Content:** 3-5 short videos (15-60 seconds)
- Product demo (fast-paced, trendy)
- Customer testimonial (authentic)
- "Before/After" scenario
- Q&A with product team
- Educational tip

**Specs:**
- Trending audio (where appropriate)
- Native TikTok format (vertical)
- On-screen text/captions
- Hashtag challenge (optional)

### 4. Paid Advertising Content

#### LinkedIn Ads
**Format:** Sponsored content ads
**Variations:** 3-5 different headline/image combinations
**Content:**
- Headline: Hook (benefit or problem statement)
- Body: 2-sentence value prop
- Image: Product or benefit-focused visual
- CTA: "Learn More" or "Sign Up"

**Targeting:** Role, industry, company size (personalized)

#### Google Search Ads
**Format:** Search text ads
**Keywords:** 20-30 high-intent keywords
**Ad copy variations:** 3-5 headlines, 2-3 descriptions
**Content:** Address search intent (problem + solution)

#### Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram)
**Format:** Image and video ads
**Variations:** 5-8 different creative combinations
**Content:**
- Primary audience: Existing customers
- Secondary audience: Look-alike prospects
- Tertiary audience: Interest targeting (competitive products)
- Message: Benefit-focused, social proof

### 5. Supporting Assets

#### Webinar/Demo
**Format:** Live webinar or on-demand video (30-45 min)  
**Content:**
- Problem overview (5 min)
- Product demo (15-20 min)
- Use cases (5-10 min)
- Q&A (10-15 min)
- Offer/CTA

**Format:** Recorded for on-demand viewing

#### Case Study
**Format:** PDF + web page (2-3 pages)  
**Content:**
- Customer overview (industry, size)
- Challenge (what problem they faced)
- Solution (how product helped)
- Results (metrics: time saved, revenue, efficiency)
- Quote from customer
- Implementation details
- Lessons learned

#### One-Pager/Datasheet
**Format:** Visual PDF (1 page)  
**Content:**
- What it is (headline)
- Key benefits (3-5 visual callouts)
- Key features (5-8 bullets)
- Pricing or availability
- CTA

### 6. Visual Design System

**Brand Assets:**
- Product hero shot (high quality image/video)
- 5-10 benefit icons or illustrations
- Color palette (primary + 2-3 accents)
- Typography system
- Logo variations

**Templates:**
- Email templates (2-3 variations)
- Social graphics (multiple sizes)
- LinkedIn document template
- Ad creative templates
- Landing page sections

## Distribution Plan

### Week 1: Pre-Launch (5 days before)
- Soft launch to employees and advisors
- Beta access to key influencers
- Press embargoes out to media
- Landing page live but unlisted
- Email sequences scheduled
- Social content calendar created

### Launch Day (Day 1)
- **10:00 AM:** Press release distributed
- **10:30 AM:** Blog post published
- **11:00 AM:** Landing page goes live (announcement)
- **11:30 AM:** Launch email sent to list
- **2:00 PM:** LinkedIn announcement posted
- **3:00 PM:** Twitter launch thread
- **4:00 PM:** Instagram/TikTok posts
- **Evening:** All hands celebration

### Week 1-2 (Days 2-14)
- Daily social media engagement
- Email series starts (day 4)
- Webinar offered (day 7)
- Paid ads ramp up (day 3-4)
- Press coverage monitoring
- Influencer outreach and sharing

### Week 3-4 (Days 15-28)
- Continue email nurture sequence
- Maintenance social posting (2-3x/week)
- Paid campaigns optimization
- Case study publication
- Follow-up with sales team
- Analytics review and adjustments

### Week 5-6 (Follow-up)
- Post-launch email: "Still interested?"
- Referral campaign (existing users)
- Update launch assets with testimonials
- Plan next phase content

## Success Criteria

### Pre-Launch QA
- [ ] Landing page fully functional, all links tested
- [ ] Email templates tested across email clients
- [ ] Social content reviewed for brand consistency
- [ ] Press release finalized and embargoed
- [ ] Analytics tracking configured
- [ ] All approvals obtained (legal, brand, leadership)

### Content Quality
- [ ] All copy error-free and on-brand
- [ ] Visuals professionally designed
- [ ] Video content engaging and high-quality
- [ ] Messaging consistent across channels
- [ ] CTAs clear and action-oriented

### Launch Execution
- [ ] All content published on schedule (100%)
- [ ] No technical issues on launch day
- [ ] Response team available for engagement
- [ ] Monitoring dashboard live

### Performance Targets (4 weeks)
- [ ] Reach: 500K+ impressions
- [ ] Engagement: 5%+ rate on social
- [ ] Email: 25%+ open rate, 3%+ CTR
- [ ] Landing page: 2%+ conversion rate
- [ ] Leads generated: >100 MQLs
- [ ] Press mentions: 10+ articles

### Longer-term (8 weeks)
- [ ] Revenue targets met
- [ ] Sales pipeline impact measured
- [ ] Customer adoption rate on target
- [ ] Brand awareness lift confirmed (survey)
- [ ] ROI >3x investment
